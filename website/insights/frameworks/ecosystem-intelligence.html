Four-Layer Ecosystem Model

Comprehensive framework for understanding ecosystem complexity across multiple interconnected layers.

1

Core Ecosystem

Direct Relationships

Immediate stakeholders with direct business relationships and regular interaction patterns.

Key Components:
  • Direct customers and clients
  • Key suppliers and vendors
  • Strategic partners and allies
  • Core team members and departments
  • Primary investors and funders
2

Extended Ecosystem

Indirect Influence

Secondary stakeholders who influence outcomes through indirect relationships and network effects.

Key Components:
  • Industry associations and networks
  • Regulatory bodies and government
  • Media and thought leaders
  • Academic and research institutions
  • Competitor networks and alliances
3

Market Ecosystem

Systemic Forces

Broader market forces and systemic influences that shape the operating environment.

Key Components:
  • Economic trends and cycles
  • Technology evolution patterns
  • Social and cultural shifts
  • Political and regulatory changes
  • Global market dynamics
4

Future Ecosystem

Emerging Patterns

Emerging trends and future possibilities that will reshape ecosystem dynamics over time.

Key Components:
  • Emerging technologies and innovations
  • Next-generation competitors
  • Evolving customer expectations
  • New regulatory frameworks
  • Disruptive business models
